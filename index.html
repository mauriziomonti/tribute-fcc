A digital nomad journey, from house-sitting to guardianship.

A short story about becoming a digital nomad.

Intro.

It all started with a three day house-sit in a small town called Lindfield, what followed was a journey from a flat in Twickenham, southwest London, to full time house-sitter, i.e. no fix address. We are constantly asked how we do this, how difficult it is, where to find houses and what are the benefits in house-sitting on a permanent, full time basis.

That is travelling between places, getting to know an area, a town, a region, understanding the vibes, meeting different people and getting to live in different urban or countryside surroundings.

Sometimes they can be remote like farmhouses in the middle of fields or mountains, other times apartments or bungalows near a beach, or small tiny apartments in the middle of London city. It's the chance to be an outsider coming into contact with the locals first-hand.

It’s is not just about saving money, but it is an important aspect. Afterall, would you consider house-sitting if costs weren't significantly lower? It was also made possible by my flexible job. At the beginning it needed to be commutable from London once or twice a week. I then quit my job, and at that point I started freelancing from anywhere.

From our experience we can tell there are positive and less positive aspects, but the overall purpose is the experience itself.

But most importantly, you have to love animals and love caring for them. You wouldn’t be there if it wasn’t for the pets. I can’t stress this enough.

As house-sits ran dry and disappeared during the pandemic, I turned to guardianship for flexible and cheap accommodation and volunteering in a local farm.

All the above hss been an awesome experience that led to the application and admission to the School Of Code bootcamp!
